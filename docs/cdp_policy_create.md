# Creating CDP Policies

In this article

To protect VMs with CDP, you must configure a CDP policy. The CDP policy defines which VMs to protect, where to store replicas, how often create short-term and long-term restore points, and so on. One CDP policy can process one or multiple VMs.

To create a CDP policy use the New CDP Policy wizard.

1. [Check prerequisites](cdp_policy_byb.md).
2. [Launch the New CDP Policy wizard](cdp_policy_launch.md).
3. [Specify a job name and advanced settings](cdp_policy_name.md).
4. [Select VMs to replicate](cdp_policy_workloads.md).
5. [Exclude objects from being replicated](cdp_policy_exclude.md).
6. [Specify the VM processing order](cdp_policy_order.md).
7. [Select a destination for replicas](cdp_policy_destination.md).
8. [Configure network mapping](cdp_policy_network.md).
9. [Configure re-IP rules](cdp_policy_reip.md).
10. [Configure seeding and mapping](cdp_policy_seeding_mapping.md).
11. [Specify data transfer and replica settings](cdp_policy_data_transfer.md).
12. [Specify notification settings](cdp_policy_notification.md).
13. [Configure a replication schedule](cdp_policy_schedule.md).
14. [Specify guest processing settings](cdp_policy_guest.md).
15. [Finish working with the wizard](cdp_policy_summary.md).
